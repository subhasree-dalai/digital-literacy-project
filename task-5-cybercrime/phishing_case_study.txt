Phishing is one of the most common types of cybercrime where attackers trick users into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, OTPs, or bank details. A common example is receiving fake emails or messages that appear to be from banks or trusted organizations.

In one such case, a user received a message claiming that their bank account would be blocked unless they verified their details immediately. The message included a link to a fake website that looked identical to the official bank website. The user entered their login credentials and OTP, after which money was withdrawn from their account.

This incident highlights the importance of being cautious while dealing with online messages and links. Cybercriminals often create urgency to make users act without thinking.

To prevent such incidents, users should always verify the authenticity of messages and avoid clicking on suspicious links. Awareness and careful behavior are key to avoiding cyber threats.
